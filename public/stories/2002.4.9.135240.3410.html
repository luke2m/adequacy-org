The 6 part troll test is a set of six simple criteria that an account or persona must pass to avoid being deleted here at Adequacy.org. It tests for redeeming value.

1. Is it ammusing? We'll let a lot slide if it makes us chuckle. Unfortunately the Aryan King account was a crude and obvious troll. There was no wit or cleverness to it.

2. Is it controversial? We at adequacy don't shy away from controversy and don't hide controversial comments. But Aryan King wasn't controversial. Few people bothered to attack his position and even fewer bothered to defend it. Most Adequacy.org readers just ignored him.

3. Is it sincere? We'll give more slack to people who really believe stupid things than people who pretend to believe stupid things in order to cause trouble.

4. Is it daring? Does the author show courage by taking this position? Ridiculing minorities on a site that (thanks to the k5 refugees) has a predominately rich white male adolescent audience isn't courageous. It's like mocking the Amish on TV. They're not going to see it and even if they do, they're pacifists. Anonymous racial invective is the same way. Be a man, put your home address and phone number on it.

5. Did it take effort? If it's just throwaway comments without any sign of research or careful composition, it's much easier to justify deleting.

6. Is it worthless shit that doesn't belong on this site? It was.
