I don't advocate torture (none / 0) (#3)
by John Wainright on Sun Mar 10th, 2002 at 10:09:50 PM PST
Simply because as a society we are above that.
However it would be nice to inflict the exact same kind of damage on a criminal that he/she inflicted upon their victim.
Some capital punishments are thought of as torture. Perhaps they are in a way.
I read a short story once entitled "Macs".
The premise was the the body of Timothy McViegh was cloned many times so that the families of his victims each had the oppurtunity to mete justice in their own way. Chilling thoughts.
I would hate to see us degenerate into roving mobs seeking out blood. But at the same time there is no sense in keeping someone around who commits these kinds of acts.
Life without parole would seem more torture than execution.
If you have no chance of leaving prison what good are you as a person?
