The trick (none / 0) (#4)
by Anonymous Reader on Sat Jul 27th, 2002 at 05:10:26 PM PST
Stand up a stick of butter lengthwise, then press the corncob onto the butter and rotate it to cover the whole circumfrence of the cob. Move the corncob back and forth as you go to ensure even coverage. Be sure to have a healthy supply of butter on hand as you go through a lot of butter using this technique.


 
How sad. (5.00 / 3) (#5)
by RobotSlave on Sat Jul 27th, 2002 at 05:13:03 PM PST
I thought I'd never see the day when a Midwesterner was raised without learning how to properly butter corn. I guess it's true: globalization is destroying local indiginous cultures everywhere.

Take a piece of bread. Put a bit of butter on the bread. Use the bread to apply the butter to the corn. This will both butter the corn and soften the butter, which can then be spread evenly over the piece of bread.

And to think this sort of aboriginal wisdom was part of a living culture only twenty years ago. It makes my heart ache, I tell you.
