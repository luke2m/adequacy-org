Re:Hi Hemos (Score:0)
by Anonymous Coward on Wednesday February 28, @11:57AM EST (#256)
'k, i'll bite.

First, there are certain ads that I despise and will not click, ever. I would rather take 2 seconds and find a competitor who didn't put up such a stupid ad for my business if i really need the service. Okay, here are my no-nos:

(1)Anything that might give me a seizure (no pallette cycling) If you are advertising something that flashes, make sure only the product flashes, not the whole ad.

(2)Anything that insults my intelligence (ie, click here to WIN WIN WIN!!!#%@%$).

(3) Anything that 'dares' me to click, or warns of impending doom if I do not. If this service/product is really something I might actually need, state WHY I need it so bad, don't just tell me I need it.

(4) ads that are change really fast, for a reason other than smooth animation.

(5) that stupid "punch the monkey ad" ... for many reasons. It's a "click 2 win" ad, and I don't waste my money on lotto, nor my time on these ads. Also, that Javascript punching glove doesn't fool me... turn the banner into a table instead, maybe you'll fool another geek into thinking that where they click influences their luck. I meanwhile, realize my chances are the same as every other schmuck on the web..."lousy".

(6) Any ad I think will benefit a company or organization I hate. This probably goes without saying... on the flipside, if the banner is for a linux App, putting a mouseover banner that says "M$ Bl0wz!" would definitely garner my attention, and probably result in a click (unless every ad starts saying the same thing...) Also, I click ads for things I know I won't buy, IF I think it will lend support to a group/person I like.

PS: I didn't tell you my entire method, that will cost you $100 US currency. And I didn't tell you this much out of the goodness of my heart; I told you what I think because I think it MIGHT have an impact on the ads I see when I surf /. and kuro5hin. I surf a lot on PC's i'm not allowed to reconfigure (e.g. with junkbuster) and that's the main reason I view ads at all.
