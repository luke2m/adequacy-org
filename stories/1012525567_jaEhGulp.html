Ben at the waist (none / 0) (#11)
by sublingual on Wed Feb 6th, 2002 at 10:27:59 AM PST
Let's take this name in three parts, and let's start in the middle. "Bin." That's wrong on the face of it. It's not "ben"; that is a transliteration of Hebrew, not Arabic. But it's not "bin" either. Most scholars would settle for "Ibn." That's the best we have in English. "Laden" doesn't cut it either. That's like the Goldfarbs coming to the U.S. and changing their name to Green, or Gold, or Greengrass even. The best transliteration would be "Ladn," possibly "Lad'n" or maybe even "Ladin," as in "Alladin," which is short for "Ali al-Din." Let's choose that last option and review. We now have "Ibn al-Din," which would translate in Arabic as "the son of the second." Yes, that's the meaning of his family name. I'd wage a holy war and besmirch my family heritage if that were my name too. Now, onto that troublesome "osama-usama-sama-sama-hey-sama-ree-bop" business. In Arabic, Mosama (or Musama) means "smiling man," or "man of smiles." Let's say that that shortens into "'Usama" or "'Osama." There's no standing convention for whether to choose o or u in these cases--Arabic has no official vowels, only diacritical marks that guide the reader/speaker. So let's just can the vowel entirely and call the dude 'Sama, or Samy for short. So "Samy Ibn al-Din" (or Smiley, Son of the Second) is how I'd spell his name.
