I am adrift in a sea of uncertainty. I do not know who I am. I do not know who my fellow Adequacy users are. I do not know why I read this site. I do not know why I am an editor here. I do not know why some of our readers return again and again. I am completely incapable of applying a bit of intellect to help unravel this conundrum.

This site has been up for over a year now, and so far I haven't been able to develop even one single shred of a theorem as to why this all exists. This is where I need your help.

I don't believe anyone who has been around here for a while is qualified to help me out. This task calls for fresh blood. What I would so love to see is a new user emerge from the woodwork and, after reviewing the place for a week or two, provide me with a complete, thorough analysis of Adequacy.

I firmly believe that this sort of insight is exactly what is called for. Some of you may believe that it had all been settled many months ago, or perhaps that it was never even an issue in the first place. To you I say, "Horse feathers!". We crave this type of expose like a priest craves little boys.

So I hereby put forth my plea: Please oh please tell me what this place is all about and, if possible, tell me why those who frequent it do so. I can't handle the amibguity of something just chugging along under its own impetus without anyone ever feeling the need to discuss the hows or whys of it all.

And if you want to be extra helpful, send me a fine bottle of Polish potato vodka.

Blame Osama (none / 0) (#9)
by First Incision on Tue Aug 6th, 2002 at 06:47:47 PM PST
I did not post immediately, because I am even older than Mr. Isn't. But I came to this site in the aftermath of 9/11. I came to Adequacy because my life was making too much sense.

Last September, I was confused. While the actual world had gone crazy, my own life was startlingly sane. Everybody in the "traditional" media was trying to make sense of it all. I knew they were attempting the impossible. A young man following the straight and narrow path for a lifetime entering medical school, that makes sense. Two smoking skyscrapers and a wounded Pentagon does not.

Then, by pure chance, I heard of Adequacy: News for Grownups. I thought maybe these grownups could help me. They could.

Of the heaps of journalism spewed towards me in the months of September, only the stories on Adequacy truly spoke to me. I strongly suggest newcomers read such classics as Kill Yr Idols: Usamah bin Muhammad bin Laden and Kill Yr Idols: God.
