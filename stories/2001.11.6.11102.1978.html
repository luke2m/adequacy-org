A friend recently got a wonderful book, The Good Citizen's Handbook: A Guide to Proper Behavior. It is chock full of good government advice from the 1st half of the 20th century, and was compiled by Jennifer McKnight-Trontz. This is an amazing guide, and I think it would be very popular among anyone who considers themselves to be Adequate. Sadly, I don't have enough free time (and probably not enough skill) to write a review that does this book justice. Would one of our distinguished contributers be interested in extolling the wonders of this book?
